Understanding Developmental Milestones

Developmental milestones are key indicators of a child’s growth. Here’s a quick guide based on CDC milestones from 2 months and beyond.
Milestones by Age

2 Months

Starts to smile, looks at parents, begins to follow things with eyes.

Learn more from CDC

4 Months

Holds head steady, pushes down on legs when feet are on a hard surface.

Learn more from CDC

6 Months

Begins to sit without support, rolls over in both directions.

Learn more from CDC

9 Months

Stands, holding on, can get into sitting position, sits without support.

Learn more from CDC

12 Months

Pulls up to stand, walks holding on to furniture, can take a few steps without holding on.

Learn more from CDC

15 Months

Walks alone, may walk up steps and run.
